Curator's note

Han Liang
Director of Programs

2026 · 04 · 14
Shanghai

The question of Pollen Index is not whether the machine made it. The machine made hundreds. The question is which hundred-and-forty-second the artist kept, which thirty-eight percent she cut, and why a horizon line, once removed, becomes a kind of dictionary entry.

Tanaka does not hide the model. She does not disguise her edits. She publishes both, alongside the work, in the manner that a 17th-century printmaker would publish the plate and the press. The artifact is not the image — it is the full trace: the photograph from Kyoto, the prompt rewritten fourteen times, the cropping decision, the twelve gold dots painted at three in the morning. Each step is visible. Each step is part of the work.

This is what Ostraka exists to make legible. An ostrakon, in ancient Athens, was a fragment of pottery on which a citizen would inscribe a name — a vote, a memory, a small act of authorship. Our gallery returns to that gesture: every work, named and traced; every collaborator, visible.

Pollen Index sold its first edition in eleven minutes. The artist requested that the second edition be held for two months. We have honoured that request.

Han Liang · for the Studio
